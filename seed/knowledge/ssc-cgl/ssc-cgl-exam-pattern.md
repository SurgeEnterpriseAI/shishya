# SSC CGL — Exam pattern (Tier 1 and Tier 2)

> Provenance: Shishya-curated summary of the scheme of examination in the
> SSC CGL 2026 notification (ssc.gov.in, 21 May 2026), cross-verified
> against multiple independent publishers on 12 June 2026. The official
> notification PDF is canonical.

## Selection flow

1. Tier 1 — computer-based test, **qualifying only** (marks do not count in
   the final merit; you must clear the cut-off to reach Tier 2).
2. Tier 2 — computer-based test, **decides the final merit**.
3. Document verification and, for some posts, a physical/skill requirement.

There is no interview in SSC CGL.

## Tier 1 pattern

- Mode: online CBT. Duration: 60 minutes total. 100 questions, 200 marks.
- Negative marking: **−0.50 marks per wrong answer** (each question carries
  2 marks).
- New from the 2026 cycle: **fixed sectional timing** — each section has its
  own 15-minute block; you cannot move between sections freely. (Verify the
  exact per-section timing in the official notification before the exam.)

| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| General Intelligence & Reasoning | 25 | 50 | 15 min |
| General Awareness | 25 | 50 | 15 min |
| Quantitative Aptitude | 25 | 50 | 15 min |
| English Comprehension | 25 | 50 | 15 min |
| Total | 100 | 200 | 60 min |

- Languages: question paper in Hindi and English (except the English
  Comprehension section, which is in English only).
- PwBD candidates eligible for a scribe get compensatory time.

## Tier 2 pattern — Paper 1 (compulsory for all posts)

Paper 1 is conducted in two sessions on the same day. Scored modules carry
**3 marks per question with −1 per wrong answer**.

### Session 1 — scored sections

| Section | Module | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Section 1 | Module I: Mathematical Abilities | 30 | 90 | 1 hour for the |
| Section 1 | Module II: Reasoning & General Intelligence | 30 | 90 | whole section |
| Section 2 | Module I: English Language & Comprehension | 45 | 135 | 1 hour for the |
| Section 2 | Module II: General Awareness | 25 | 75 | whole section |

Scored total: 130 questions, 390 marks.

### Qualifying modules (must pass, do not add to merit)

| Module | Content | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Section 3, Module I | Computer Knowledge Test (20 questions, 60 marks) | 15 min |
| Section 3, Module II (Session 2) | Data Entry Speed Test (DEST) — ~2000 key depressions in 15 min | 15 min |

- The Computer Knowledge module and DEST are **qualifying**: SSC sets a
  minimum standard; failing either disqualifies the candidate even with a
  high scored total.
- DEST is mandatory for all posts (exemptions exist for certain PwBD
  categories per the notification).

## Tier 2 pattern — Paper 2 (JSO / Statistical Investigator only)

- Subject: Statistics. 100 questions, 200 marks, 2 hours.
- Marking: 2 marks per correct answer, **−0.50 per wrong answer**.
- Only candidates who applied for Junior Statistical Officer or Statistical
  Investigator Grade-II posts and meet the special eligibility take Paper 2.

## Tier 2 pattern — Paper 3 (AAO posts only, introduced for this cycle)

- Subject: General Studies (Finance and Economics). 100 questions,
  200 marks, 2 hours.
- Marking: 2 marks per correct answer, **−0.50 per wrong answer**.
- Only candidates who opted for Assistant Audit Officer / Assistant Accounts
  Officer posts take Paper 3.

## Strategy implications of the pattern

- Tier 1 is qualifying — the goal is clearing the cut-off safely, not
  maximising the score. Do not burn months over-optimising Tier 1 topics at
  the expense of Tier 2 depth.
- Tier 2 Paper 1 decides rank. Mathematical Abilities and English carry the
  largest scored weight (90 + 135 = 225 of 390).
- Negative marking differs by stage: −0.50 in Tier 1 vs −1 in Tier 2
  Paper 1 scored modules. Guessing thresholds should be more conservative
  in Tier 2.
- With sectional timing in Tier 1, per-section speed practice matters; you
  cannot bank time from a strong section for a weak one.
- Do not neglect the qualifying modules: every year candidates with strong
  scores are eliminated by the DEST or computer module.
