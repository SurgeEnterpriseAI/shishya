# SSC CGL — Syllabus overview by section

> Provenance: Shishya-curated rendering of the indicative syllabus in the
> SSC CGL notification (ssc.gov.in). The notification's syllabus is
> indicative, not an exhaustive topic list; SSC can ask anything within the
> stated areas. Cross-verified 12 June 2026.

## Quantitative Aptitude (Tier 1) / Mathematical Abilities (Tier 2)

- Number systems: whole numbers, decimals, fractions, relationships between
  numbers, LCM and HCF.
- Arithmetic: percentages, ratio and proportion, square roots, averages,
  interest (simple and compound), profit and loss, discount, partnership,
  mixture and alligation, time and distance, time and work.
- Algebra: basic algebraic identities, linear equations and their graphs.
- Geometry: triangles (centres, congruence, similarity), circles (chords,
  tangents, angles), quadrilaterals, regular polygons.
- Mensuration: triangle, quadrilaterals, regular polygons, circle, right
  prism, right circular cone, right circular cylinder, sphere, hemispheres,
  rectangular parallelepiped, pyramids.
- Trigonometry: trigonometric ratios, complementary angles, heights and
  distances, standard identities.
- Statistics & data interpretation: tables and graphs (histograms,
  frequency polygon, bar diagram, pie chart), mean, median, mode, standard
  deviation.

## General Intelligence & Reasoning

- Verbal: analogies, classification (odd one out), series (semantic, number,
  figural), coding–decoding, blood relations, direction sense, order and
  ranking, syllogism, statement–conclusion, word formation, dictionary
  arrangement, calendar problems.
- Non-verbal: figural analogy and classification, figural series, mirror and
  water images, paper folding and cutting, embedded figures, figure
  completion, dice and cubes, Venn diagrams.
- Other: matrix-based questions, mathematical operations, problem solving,
  emotional and social intelligence (occasionally).

## English Comprehension (Tier 1) / English Language & Comprehension (Tier 2)

- Vocabulary: synonyms, antonyms, one-word substitution, idioms and phrases,
  commonly misspelt words.
- Grammar: spotting errors, fill in the blanks, improvement of sentences,
  active/passive voice, direct/indirect narration.
- Usage: cloze passages, para jumbles (sentence rearrangement).
- Reading comprehension passages (Tier 2 carries a heavier comprehension
  load than Tier 1).

## General Awareness

- India and its neighbouring countries: history, culture, geography,
  economic scene, general polity, scientific research.
- Static GK: important dates, books and authors, awards, sports, art and
  culture.
- Science: everyday science across physics, chemistry, biology at a general
  level.
- Current affairs: national and international events, government schemes,
  appointments, summits — typically from the 9–12 months before the exam.

## Tier 2 qualifying modules

- Computer Knowledge: computer basics (CPU, memory, I/O), software
  (Windows, MS Office basics), internet and email, networking basics, and
  cyber security basics (malware, common attacks, preventive practices).
- Data Entry Speed Test: typing a given English passage; speed standard of
  about 2000 key depressions in 15 minutes.

## Paper 2 (JSO) — Statistics

Collection and representation of data, measures of central tendency and
dispersion, moments, skewness and kurtosis, correlation and regression,
probability theory, random variables and distributions, sampling theory,
statistical inference, analysis of variance, time series, index numbers.

## Paper 3 (AAO) — General Studies (Finance & Economics)

Fundamentals of accounting (financial accounting basics, single/double
entry, books of original entry, bank reconciliation, trial balance,
rectification of errors, final accounts, depreciation, valuation of
inventories), and economics & governance (Comptroller and Auditor General,
Finance Commission, basic micro and macro economics, forms of market,
national income, money and banking, role of information technology in
governance).
