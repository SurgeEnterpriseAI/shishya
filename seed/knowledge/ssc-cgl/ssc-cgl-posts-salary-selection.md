# SSC CGL — Posts, pay levels, and what happens after the exam

> Provenance: Shishya-curated summary based on the SSC CGL notification's
> post table (ssc.gov.in) and the 7th CPC pay matrix. Cross-verified
> 12 June 2026. Post-wise vacancy counts change during the cycle — always
> check the latest vacancy annexure on ssc.gov.in.

## What CGL recruits for

CGL fills Group B and Group C posts across central ministries, departments,
and constitutional bodies — roughly 40 post categories in a typical cycle.

## Pay levels (7th CPC matrix, basic pay range)

- Level 8 (₹47,600–1,51,100): Assistant Audit Officer and Assistant
  Accounts Officer (under C&AG) — the highest-paying CGL posts; gazetted,
  Group B. Requires Tier 2 Paper 3.
- Level 7 (₹44,900–1,42,400): Assistant Section Officer (Central
  Secretariat Service, MEA, AFHQ, etc.), Assistant in various ministries,
  Inspector of Income Tax, Inspector (Central Excise / Preventive Officer /
  Examiner) in CBIC, Assistant Enforcement Officer (ED), Sub-Inspector
  (CBI), Inspector of Posts, Junior Statistical Officer (requires Paper 2),
  Divisional Accountant.
- Level 6 (₹35,400–1,12,400): Assistant/Superintendent in other
  departments, Sub-Inspector (NIA), Statistical Investigator Grade-II.
- Level 5 (₹29,200–92,300): Auditor (C&AG, CGDA), Accountant, Junior
  Accountant.
- Level 4 (₹25,500–81,100): Senior Secretariat Assistant / Upper Division
  Clerk, Tax Assistant (CBDT/CBIC).

Gross salary adds DA, HRA (city-dependent), TA and other allowances to the
basic pay — in-hand for a Level 7 post in a metro is typically in the
₹55,000–65,000/month range at entry (varies with allowances in force).

## How posts are allotted

- Final merit is prepared from Tier 2 scored marks.
- During option filling, candidates rank their post preferences; allotment
  follows merit + preference + category + post-specific requirements.
- Some posts have extra requirements: physical standards/tests (e.g.
  Inspector Central Excise, SI in CBI/NIA), and JSO/AAO need their special
  papers.

## After the result

1. Document verification by the user department.
2. Medical examination / physical tests where applicable.
3. Dossier allotment to the department; joining letters follow the
   department's own timeline (can take months after the final result).

## Honest context for aspirants

- Competition: lakhs of applicants compete for ~12k posts; realistic
  preparation windows are 6–12 months of consistent study.
- Posting location depends on the department and post; all-India service
  liability applies to most posts.
- Career growth differs sharply by department (e.g. Income Tax Inspector →
  ITO → Assistant Commissioner over ~15–20 years via departmental exams;
  ASO in CSS has its own promotion ladder). Choose preferences on the
  career ladder you want, not only the entry pay level.
